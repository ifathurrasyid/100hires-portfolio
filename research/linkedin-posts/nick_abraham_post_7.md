I don't care how good AI gets. Nobody will buy a 12-month, $500,000-per-year deal from an AI agent. 

I keep seeing this narrative that vendor decisions will be made agent-to-agent, and I just don’t buy it, for a few reasons:

1. I get that AI is great, but is it great enough to trust it with $500K+ of budget and 0 human oversight? No.

2. I’m sure it can help evaluate vendors, but weighing purchase decisions like is something you want humans to be more available to do, because of the complexities involved in the deal.

3. Most importantly: the second we get to a point where agents are buying from other agents, we’ll see a whole new industry of “agent gamification” arise (i.e. I tell my agent what to say to make yours buy from mine), leading to a whole host of legal disputes.

There’s lots of change coming, but ENT sellers will not get the brunt of it.
