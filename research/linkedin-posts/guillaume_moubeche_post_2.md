Most founders pick a lane.

Bootstrap or raise. 

Stay in control or go big. 

Be the King or get Rich.

Jason Cohen picked both.

And built 2 unicorns to prove it.

First, he bootstrapped WP Engine to millions in profit with zero funding. Sold it in 2007. Unicorn status by 2009.

Then he did it again but this time raised $250M from Silver Lake.

By 2024? $400M revenue. 200,000 customers.

Two unicorns. Two completely opposite playbooks.

We talk a lot about how to grow a company but no one really talks about the behind the scenes.

It's easy to know if you're a great entrepreneur but how do you know if you're a great CEO?

And most importantly, are you the right CEO at all different stages?

It takes a lot of self awareness to step down from the CEO role and it's never as easy as people might think.

I asked Jason a lot of questions I see most founders avoid publicly and he answers very honestly.

Some of his answers surprised me.
