A lot of what we call anxiety is mind-made.

A prospect ghosts.

And the mind instantly says:

“They’re not interested.”
“I blew it.”
“I’m losing my edge.”

But that’s not what happened.

What happened is:

They didn’t reply.

Everything after that is story.

The event is real.
The meaning is made up.

That’s where suffering begins.

Not in the silence.

In the story we attach to the silence.

The best sellers I know don’t carry one call into the next.

They don’t let the mind turn information into identity.

A no is information.
Silence is information.
Pushback is information.

Nothing more.

The mind says:
“This always happens.”

Your center says:
“This happened.”

Big difference.

Don’t let one moment become a story about who you are.
