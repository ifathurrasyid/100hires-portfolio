The product lead for internal ai at Ramp PERFECTLY explained why a Centralized > Decentralized AI strategy is the difference between AI Native & wasting Claude's time.

If you haven't checked out the article on X, "We Built Every Employee at Ramp Their Own AI Coworker" - this is your sign that you should.

They built what they're calling "Glass". It's the actualization of Jack Dorsey essay, "From Hierarchy to Intelligence".

You see, I've been trying to explain this to folks... building in Claude is fun. I do it all the time.

But, if I build something... how can I make sure everyone has access to what I'm doing/learning etc.?

How can I benefit from the connectors, scripts, and skills already built by others?

Also... how can I ensure I'm building something that isn't so brittle that I need a write a new script every time I want to run a new workflow?

Building AI internally *IS* a product.

Having a bunch of Claude spaghetti code floating around is a governance nightmare... it's also just not very efficient.

Letting a thousands flowers come to bloom WAS a great strategy when we were still deciding the best way to bring AI to life within our companies.

But, with the rise of harnessing ai (think OpenClaw)... this really needs to be a top down initiative. 

A core AI owner. Department extensions of that AI owner.

It's not just GTM engineering. It's back office. It's HR, its finance... its even engineering.

Internal AI is a product that creates a differentiated, compounding advantage for you.

It's also a dope recruiting tool haha... like do you want to work at the company that's maximizing their use of AI... or their competitor who isn't?

The release of most recent Opus model in February was the starting gun.

Ramp is doing the best job advertising their progress.
We've built a very similar program to Glass internally.

It's not as snazzy... we're smaller - doesn't need to be.
(We definitely need to add some of the functionality they've built around Dojo to share skills more easily)

Ramp is an AI-Native company.
I'd argue this is the new standard of excellence.
